A cross-cultural comparison of Video Storytelling in Advertising

based on: Ben Aicha, A., & Bouzaabia, R. (2023). The effects of video storytelling advertising on consumers’ online reactions on Facebook: a cross-cultural study. Qualitative Market Research: An International Journal, 26(3), 247–268

Introduction

Cross-cultural digital storytelling has grown in relevance, yet little attention has been given to how different cultures emotionally and cognitively react to the same storytelling advertisement on social media.

This study investigates how consumers from different cultural backgrounds respond to the same storytelling advertisement on social media. The case analyzed is Dove’s globally known “Real Beauty Sketches” campaign, which uses emotional storytelling to challenge beauty standards.

By comparing audience reactions in France and Tunisia, the study explores how cultural values shape the interpretation and emotional impact of storytelling elements. The research is grounded in Hofstede’s cultural dimensions.

Methodology

Figure 2: Research process

Two qualitative netnographic studies were conducted.  Grounded Theory guided data collection and coding.

Participants: Approx. 86 comments per country (n ≈ 172 total)

Video: Dove’s “Real Beauty Sketches” with culturally adapted versions (language & context)

Story components analyzed:
•Plot (narrative structure and message)
•Characters (women and artist)
•Verisimilitude (realistic feel, documentary style)

Data were analyzed in NVivo using open, axial, and selective coding. Themes were developed into conceptual frameworks per country.

Cognitive reactions dominant:
• Viewers reflected on the message and societal norms.

Affective and behavioral responses minimal:
• Emotional resonance was rare, and sharing or endorsement behavior almost absent.

Verisimilitude was not a significant factor for emotional engagement. Characters were interpreted analytically:
Some viewers felt the characters were not representative of local realities.
Self-confidence was expressed more as rational affirmation than emotional impact.

Cognitive, affective, and behavioral responses observed:
• Plot evoked empathy, tears, and identification
• Characters were seen as relatable and inspirational
• Verisimilitude (realism) increased credibility and emotional immersion

Behavioral engagement: Many viewers liked, shared, or commented, inviting others to watch.

Conclusion

The study highlights how culture influences emotional and cognitive engagement with storytelling in advertising.
While the Dove campaign uses the same narrative across cultures, it produces different responses:

In Tunisia, the video functions as a tool for rational reflection and collective interpretation. The story is valued for its message, but remains emotionally distant.

In France, the same video evokes personal emotions, deeper identification, and social interaction.
This suggests that universal storytelling does not lead to universal impact. Effective global advertising must consider emotional norms, cultural values, and local interpretations of narrative devices like authenticity and character design.

❗Limitations:
While demographic data was collected, it was not directly incorporated into the analysis. Therefore, interpretations remain at a cultural level without exploring variation by age, gender, or background.

What this means for educators and designers

Storytelling needs cultural sensitivity

A campaign or learning tool that emotionally moves one audience may leave another untouched. Emotional resonance depends heavily on cultural expectations, norms, and values. Designers should test and adapt content for different target cultures to avoid one-size-fits-all messaging.

Cognitive vs. emotional engagement

While some users respond with rational reflection, others connect emotionally and engage socially. Effective design should combine both cognitive and affective elements. This ensures meaningful interaction across different cultures and learning preferences.

Use realism and characters wisely

Realistic settings and relatable characters can enhance emotional impact—but only when they reflect local realities. If characters appear foreign or inauthentic, users may disconnect. Culturally grounded storytelling creates stronger emotional ties than generalized global narratives.
